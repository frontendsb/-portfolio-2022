Hooghly Bridge

Vidyasagar Setu is the longest cable-stayed bridge in India and
one of the longest in Asia with a total length of 823 metres (2,700 ft).
It was the second bridge builds across the Hooghly River.

Construction began on 3 July 1979, and the bridge was commissioned on 10 October
1992 by the Hooghly River Bridge Commission. The bridge is used by around
30,000 vehicles daily, considerably less than the bridge's capacity of 85,000.

Hooghly Bridge

The best way to tour the bridge is to walk across the bridge on foot
alternatively you could also take a ferry ride towards BAGHBAZAR
area & cross the bridge underneath.

From here, you can get a bird's eye view of the bustling city: the busy
dockyard in the middle of the Ganges, serene boats cruising below the
bridge and ongoing pujas, all add to the appeal of the bridge. During sunsets,
as the water gleams with the colours of the sun, the view from the bridge is spectacular.
